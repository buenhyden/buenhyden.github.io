29:T1c6b,<h2 id="backend-platform-devops-engineer">Backend, Platform &#x26; DevOps Engineer</h2>
<p>I've spent most of my career building backends and platform infrastructure with Python and Node.js. My work has ranged from public-sector B2G/B2B SaaS platforms to real-time high-availability notification systems and multi-cloud SRE monitoring — all across <strong>Azure, Naver Cloud Platform (NCP), and on-premise</strong> hybrid environments. I designed and operated these systems myself, and I got good at keeping them running.</p>
<p>Early on, shipping features was the whole job. At some point, though, the question shifted: "How fast does this system come back when something breaks?" That question stuck with me. Since then, I've been obsessed with HA architecture, observability, and building solid incident response workflows.</p>
<p>I've built CI/CD pipelines on NCP DevTools (Source Commit/Build/Deploy) and GitHub Actions, operated real-time anomaly detection with Prometheus, Grafana, and Alertmanager, and put together search (OpenSearch), caching (Redis), async processing (Celery), and ETL pipelines. The through-line has always been the same: availability and performance, at the same time.</p>
<p>When we migrated a public SaaS product to NCP-based microservices, I handled the <strong>CSAP, K-PaaS, and national security certifications</strong> directly. That meant addressing security requirements at the architecture level, not just checking boxes. On the performance side, I improved our notification pipeline to cut the error rate by 75% while scaling the system to handle 5x the load. Standardizing our monitoring brought MTTR down by 30%.</p>
<p>More recently, I've been expanding from my backend and infrastructure roots into <strong>data engineering pipelines, AI/LLMOps, and Infrastructure as Code</strong>.</p>
